# Conditional Reliability of Machine-Learning Metrics

## Abstract

Problem, conditional-reliability thesis, MBE estimand, known-truth calibration,
public-corpus atlas, protected transfer, and scoped conclusion. Do not report a
global count of failed metrics as the headline result.

## 1. Introduction

- Metric papers often collapse association, incremental utility, transport,
  intervention consistency, and measurement cost.
- A metric can be predictive yet redundant with cheap information, or useful in
  one task family and misleading in another.
- Contributions: reliability profile, calibrated audit, deceptive-control
  benchmark, public atlas, and gated abstaining selector.

## 2. Related Work

- Generalization measures and Jiang-style conditional evaluation.

- Model zoos and public checkpoint corpora.

## 5. Known-Truth Calibration

- Synthetic nulls, proxies, interactions, Simpson reversal, and genuine signal.
- Shared comparison with ranks, granulated Kendall, normalized CMI, and robust
  sign error.
- Failure ledger: leakage, weak nuisance models, discarded interaction degree,
  unstable raw powers, and clustered permutation error.

## 8. Prospective Selection And Abstention

Nested leave-one-task-family-out feasibility, frozen comparators, regret,

## 9. Protected Image And Language Evidence

Corrected factorials, valid causal language modeling, frozen holdout, deviations,
and independent replication. This section remains empty until compute runs are
complete.

## 10. FIM Effective-Rank Case Study

Present the original good-faith metric proposal, ordinary evaluation success,
MBE failure, and what the self-falsification taught about metric claims.

## 11. Limitations

Learner-relative rather than universal conditional information; finite task
families; dependence assumptions; expensive metric implementations; artifact
availability; non-causal interpretation; selector maturity.

## 12. Reporting Recommendations

Require declared targets, baseline ladders, configuration-level uncertainty,
deceptive controls, task-specific profiles, implementation provenance, and
abstention where evidence is weak.
